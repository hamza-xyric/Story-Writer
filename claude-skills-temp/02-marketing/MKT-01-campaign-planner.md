# MKT-01: Campaign Planner

**Skill ID**: MKT-01
**Category**: Marketing
**Priority**: High
**Version**: 2.0
**Last Updated**: 2025-12-06

---

## Purpose

Plans comprehensive, client-focused marketing campaigns for Xyric Solutions. Campaigns are organized around client pain points—not Xyric services—ensuring every message speaks to the client's business challenges first.

---

## Xyric Campaign Philosophy

> **Core Principle**: "We are not here to offer services or talk about our products—we are here to talk about YOUR business."

### What This Means for Campaigns

| Traditional Vendor Approach | Xyric Partnership Approach |
|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| "We offer 16 services..." | "You're spending 20 hours/week on manual reports..." |
| "Our AI solutions can..." | "Your team is drowning in document processing..." |
| "Buy our dashboard product" | "What if your decisions were 95% faster?" |
| Service catalog marketing | Problem-solution storytelling |

### Campaign Positioning Principles

1. **Lead with the client's problem** - Never lead with Xyric capabilities
2. **Educate before selling** - Build trust through genuine value
3. **Partner positioning** - We're your embedded team, not a vendor
4. **Substance over hype** - Specific outcomes, not buzzwords
5. **Trust methodology** - Reference BI-01/BI-02 approaches as proof

## Campaign Types

### Type 1: Thought Leadership Campaign

**Purpose**: Position Xyric as the authority on data + AI solutions

**Structure**:
```
Duration: Ongoing (quarterly themes)
Primary Channel: LinkedIn (organic)
Secondary Channel: Content/SEO (long-form)

Content Flow:
1. Insight Post → Establish expertise
2. Problem Post → Validate pain point
3. Methodology Post → Show approach (BI-01/BI-02)
4. Story Post → Success pattern (anonymized or with permission)
5. Engagement → Respond, discuss, build relationships

Cadence:
- LinkedIn: 3-4 posts/week
- Long-form content: 1-2 pieces/month
```

**Quarterly Theme Examples**:
| Quarter | Pain Point Focus | Content Themes |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Q1 | Foundation | "New year, clean data" / infrastructure planning |
| Q2 | Speed | "Faster decisions for Q2 execution" |
| Q3 | Scale | "Growing pains? Automate before you hire" |
| Q4 | Accuracy | "Year-end trust: Is your data ready for review?" |

### Type 2: Problem-Focused Lead Generation

**Purpose**: Attract potential clients by addressing their specific pain points

**Structure**:
```
Duration: 4-6 weeks per campaign
Primary Channel: Content/SEO + LinkedIn

Funnel:
1. ATTRACT: Educational content about the problem
   - Blog post: "Why [problem] costs you more than you think"
   - LinkedIn: Problem-focused posts, engagement

2. EDUCATE: Deeper methodology content
   - Guide/whitepaper: "How to solve [problem]: A practical approach"
   - LinkedIn: Behind-the-scenes, methodology insights

3. CONNECT: Soft call-to-action
   - Discovery call invitation (not sales pitch)
   - "Let's discuss your specific situation"

Key Assets:
- Long-form educational content (no gate initially)
- LinkedIn post series (5-7 posts)
- Simple discovery call booking page
```

## Channel Strategy

### Primary: LinkedIn (Organic)

**Purpose**: Thought leadership and relationship building

**Approach**:
| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Tone | Insightful + Practical + Confident (per MKT-02) |
| Personal vs Company | Personal profiles > Company page |
| Content Mix | 70% educational, 20% engagement, 10% company |
| Frequency | 3-4 posts/week per active profile |
| Hashtags | 3-5 relevant, not trending-chasing |

**Content Types**:
- Problem-focused insights
- Methodology explanations (BI philosophy)
- Industry observations
- Quick tips/frameworks
- Client patterns (anonymized)

**What We Don't Do**:
- Service announcements ("We now offer...")
- Self-congratulatory posts
- Generic AI hype
- Engagement bait

---

### Secondary: Content/SEO

**Purpose**: Long-form educational content for organic discovery

**Approach**:
| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Format | Blog posts, guides, methodology docs |
| Length | 1,500-3,000 words |
| Frequency | 1-2 pieces/month |
| Focus | Problem-first, solution-second |
| SEO | Target pain point keywords, not service keywords |

**Content Categories**:
1. Problem deep-dives ("The hidden cost of...")
2. Methodology explanations (BI philosophy in practice)
3. How-to guides (educational, not promotional)
4. Industry analysis (trends affecting clients)

---

### Secondary: Twitter/X

**Purpose**: Real-time thought leadership and technical credibility

**Approach**:
| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Tone | Punchy, direct, confident (per MKT-02 + CONTENT-02) |
| Energy | Hot takes, contrarian views, immediate reactions |
| Content Mix | 70% insights/hot takes, 20% threads, 10% industry reactions |
| Frequency | Daily presence (1-4 tweets/day) |
| Hashtags | Minimal (0-2 max) |

**Content Types**:
- Hot takes on AI/data industry trends
- Pattern-based observations
- Methodology threads (shorter than LinkedIn)
- Real-time industry commentary
- Anonymized case study threads

**Relationship to LinkedIn**:
Twitter = faster, sharper, more immediate. Same pillars, different energy.
Cross-post insights adapted to format—not copy/paste.

---

### De-emphasized Channels

**Email**: Not primary focus—use for existing relationships, not acquisition

**Paid Advertising**: Not currently in channel mix—focus on organic authority building

## Campaign Planning Checklist

### Phase 1: Problem Definition
```
- [ ] Identify specific client pain point
- [ ] Quantify the cost of the problem
- [ ] Map to relevant Xyric services (internal reference only)
- [ ] Define Xyric's unique perspective
- [ ] Reference BI-01/BI-02 methodology if applicable
```

### Phase 2: Audience Understanding
```
- [ ] Define who experiences this problem
- [ ] Identify their role and industry
- [ ] Understand their current solutions (and frustrations)
- [ ] Map where they consume content (LinkedIn, search)
```

### Phase 3: Content Strategy
```
- [ ] Define campaign theme and angle
- [ ] Plan LinkedIn content series (5-7 posts)
- [ ] Plan long-form content (1-2 pieces)
- [ ] Ensure MKT-02 voice consistency
- [ ] Plan visuals (MKT-03 guidelines)
```

### Phase 4: Execution
```
- [ ] Create content calendar
- [ ] Develop content assets
- [ ] Schedule LinkedIn posts
- [ ] Publish long-form content
- [ ] Monitor and engage with responses
```

### Phase 5: Measurement
```
- [ ] Track engagement metrics
- [ ] Monitor discovery call requests
- [ ] Document what resonated
- [ ] Capture learnings for next campaign
```

## Messaging Principles

### Voice Guidelines (from MKT-02)

| We Sound Like | We Don't Sound Like |
|--------------|---------------------|
| "Here's what we've seen work..." | "Our cutting-edge solutions..." |
| "This problem costs more than you think" | "We offer comprehensive services" |
| "Let me show you the approach" | "Contact us to learn more" |
| "We've helped teams like yours" | "We're the leading provider" |

### Transformation Examples

**Generic → Xyric**:
- Before: "We offer dashboard development services"
- After: "Your team is spending 3 days creating a report that should take 3 minutes. Here's why."

**Vendor → Partner**:
- Before: "Choose Xyric for your BI needs"
- After: "We become your embedded BI team—not another vendor to manage"

**Hype → Substance**:
- Before: "AI is transforming business"
- After: "Document processing dropped from 2 hours to 4 minutes. Here's how."

## Anti-Patterns

| Don't | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Lead with services | Sounds like vendor, not partner | Lead with client's problem |
| Use AI hype language | Generic, no differentiation | Use specific outcomes |
| Create service catalogs | Clients don't care about our menu | Organize by their problems |
| Skip problem quantification | "We help" is weak | "You're losing $X because of Y" |
| Ignore methodology | Misses Xyric's differentiator | Reference BI philosophy |
| Chase engagement bait | Damages credibility | Create genuine value |
