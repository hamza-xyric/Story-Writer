## Xyric Communication Philosophy

| Principle | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Direct over diplomatic** | Say what you mean. Don't bury the lead. | "We missed the deadline" not "The timeline experienced some challenges" |
| **Progress over perfection** | Share early, iterate openly | Share work-in-progress, ask for input |
| **Context over assumptions** | Explain why, not just what | Include reasoning behind decisions |
| **Human over corporate** | Write like you talk | No robot-speak, no jargon soup |

## Format Guidelines

### Email Format
```
- Subject line: Specific, scannable, action-oriented
- Opening: Get to the point in first sentence
- Body: Bullet points for lists, short paragraphs
- Closing: Clear next steps or call to response
- Sign-off: Match the relationship (casual for team, professional for external)
```

### Notion Page Format
```
- Title: Clear and specific
- Quick summary at top (TL;DR)
- Use headers to structure content
- Tables for data/comparisons
- Checkboxes for action items
- Link to related pages
- Update timestamp and author
```

## Quality Checklist

Before sending:

- [ ] **Lead with the point**: Can someone understand the main message from the first sentence?
- [ ] **Scannable**: Can someone skim this and get the key info?
- [ ] **Actionable**: Are next steps and owners clear?
- [ ] **Human**: Does this sound like a person wrote it? Read it aloud.
- [ ] **Specific**: Are there concrete details, not vague statements?
- [ ] **Right audience**: Is this going to the right people? Not too many, not too few?
- [ ] **Xyric voice**: Direct, collaborative, transparent?

