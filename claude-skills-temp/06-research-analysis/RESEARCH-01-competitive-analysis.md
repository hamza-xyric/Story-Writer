## Purpose

Conduct competitive analysis to understand market positioning, identify opportunities, and inform strategic decisions for Xyric products and services.

---

## When to Activate

**Triggers**:
- "Analyze competitors for [product]"
- "Who are our main competitors?"
- "How do we compare to [competitor]?"
- Before launching new products
- During strategic planning

---

## The 5P Framework

| Dimension | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **P**ositioning | How they position themselves in market |
| **P**roduct | Features, quality, technology stack |
| **P**ricing | Price points, model (subscription, one-time, freemium) |
| **P**romotion | Marketing channels, content strategy |
| **P**eople | Team size, expertise, culture |

---

## Competitor Categories

| Type | Definition | Priority |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Direct** | Same product, same market | High |
| **Indirect** | Different product, same need | Medium |
| **Potential** | Could enter this market | Low |
| **Substitutes** | Alternative solutions | Medium |

## Xyric Rules

1. **Use multiple sources** - never rely on single source
2. **Include indirect competitors** - they may become direct
3. **Focus on actionable insights** - not just data collection
4. **Use CORE-01** - no invented competitive data
5. **Update regularly** - quarterly minimum for fast-moving markets

## Anti-Patterns

- Relying on single source
- Analyzing only direct competitors
- Ignoring small/new entrants
- Copying competitor strategy blindly
- Analysis paralysis (over-researching)
